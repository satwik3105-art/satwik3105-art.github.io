Resources

A curated list of resources I've found genuinely valuable as a student of biology and an aspiring researcher.

🧬 Molecular & Cell Biology

  • Molecular Biology of the Cell — Alberts et al. — The gold-standard textbook; freely available via NCBI Bookshelf.
  • MIT OpenCourseWare: Molecular Biology — Excellent lecture materials covering DNA, RNA, and protein.
  • Khan Academy AP Biology — Clear, concise explanations for building foundational knowledge.

🧠 Neuroscience

  • Neuroscience Online (UTHealth) — Free, comprehensive neuroscience textbook with excellent diagrams.
  • Fundamentals of Neuroscience (HarvardX) — Beautifully produced three-part introductory course.
  • Nature Neuroscience Collections — Curated research highlights for staying current.

🔬 Cancer Biology & Stem Cells

  • NCI Dictionary of Cancer Terms — Indispensable reference for navigating oncology literature.
  • ISSCR Stem Cell Resources — Curated learning materials from the leading international society.
  • Cell Stem Cell Journal — Leading journal in the field; great for reading primary literature.

💻 Bioinformatics & Coding

  • Babraham Bioinformatics Training — Free, practical tutorials in NGS and genomics analysis.
  • Kaggle Python Course — Hands-on Python for data analysis; a great starting point.
  • Bioconductor Course Materials — R-based genomics and bioinformatics workflows.

📚 Science Communication & Writing

  • How to write a first-class paper (Nature) — Practical tips directly from journal editors.
  • Writing in the Sciences (Stanford/Coursera) — Highly recommended for clear scientific writing.
