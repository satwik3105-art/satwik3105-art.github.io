About Me

Hello! I'm Satwik Mahapatra 🔬 🧬 🧠 ☕

I am a Bachelor's student at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru — one of India's most prestigious research institutions. My academic interests lie at the intersection of cancer biology, neuroscience, and stem cell research, areas that I believe hold enormous potential for transforming our understanding of human health and disease.

I am broadly fascinated by the molecular and cellular processes that govern how cells grow, differentiate, and sometimes go awry. In cancer biology, I am drawn to understanding the signalling pathways and genetic alterations that drive tumour initiation and progression. In neuroscience, I am captivated by questions about neural development, synaptic plasticity, and the molecular underpinnings of neurological disorders. My interest in stem cell research ties these threads together — particularly the potential of pluripotent stem cells as disease models and platforms for regenerative medicine.

I am currently building my foundation in experimental biology, bioinformatics, and scientific communication. I actively seek research opportunities, collaborations, and mentorship that will allow me to grow as a scientist.

Outside the lab, I enjoy reading popular science books, exploring Bengaluru's vibrant café culture, and having long conversations about the future of medicine and technology. Feel free to reach out at satwik3105@gmail.com — I'm always happy to connect!
